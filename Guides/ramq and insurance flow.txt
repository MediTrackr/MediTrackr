Yes — and that’s actually the right architecture direction for Meditrackr.

You’re basically describing a:

RAMQ billing command center

where the app:

sends claims
receives responses/reports
tracks payouts
flags issues
consolidates everything into one workflow

That’s exactly how modern RCM (revenue cycle management) systems evolve.

🧠 The important distinction

You probably won’t directly replace RAMQ itself.

Instead, Meditrackr becomes:

the workflow layer
the reconciliation layer
the intelligence layer

around RAMQ communications.

🚀 Ideal Meditrackr flow
1. Doctor submits billing

Inside Meditrackr:

Consultation
↓
RAMQ billing codes generated
↓
Claim package created
2. Claim transmitted

Could happen through:

RAMQ-supported transmission formats
existing billing gateways
fax/PDF workflow initially
future API/integration methods
3. RAMQ responses received

Your app ingests:

remittance reports
rejection notices
payment summaries
adjustment notices

through:

fax ingestion
PDFs
uploaded reports
integrations later
🧠 Then the magic happens

Meditrackr consolidates everything into statuses like:

Status	Meaning
🟢 Paid	fully reimbursed
🟡 Partial	partially paid
🔴 Rejected	denied claim
🔵 Pending	awaiting RAMQ
🟣 Review Needed	mismatch/problem

That’s the dashboard doctors actually want.

🔥 The REALLY valuable part

Not just showing:

“claim rejected”

But:

WHY it was rejected

Example:

Code incompatible with visit type
Missing modifier
Expired eligibility
Duplicate submission

That’s where automation saves huge admin time.

🏦 Payment reconciliation flow

Your app can then:

estimate expected payouts
compare against remittance totals
compare against bank deposits
flag discrepancies automatically

Example:

Expected: $14,122
Received: $13,870

→ 4 rejected claims detected

That’s very valuable operationally.

📠 Fax absolutely can still work in this model

Initially:

RAMQ
↓
Fax/PDF
↓
Meditrackr parser
↓
Structured claims/payment data

Later:

API integrations
electronic interchange
direct gateway integrations

can replace pieces gradually.

🧠 What makes this powerful

Most current clinic systems are fragmented:

one system for billing
another for documents
another for reconciliation
spreadsheets everywhere

You’re describing:

one unified operational dashboard

That’s much stronger.

⚠️ Important practical note

You’ll likely need:

a billing engine
OCR/document parsing
audit logs
role permissions
compliance workflows
reconciliation logic
